## Session Overview

The student shared plans to go hiking with a friend over the weekend. The teacher corrected the common error of inserting 'to' before a gerund in 'go + activity' phrases. The student also used the idiom 'my cup of tea' naturally and confidently.

## 💬 Idioms

### “my cup of tea”

Something you personally enjoy or that suits your taste — often used in the negative to say something is not for you.

- *Loud nightclubs are not really my cup of tea; I prefer quiet jazz bars.*
- *Mountain hiking is exactly my cup of tea — I love the fresh air and the challenge.*

### “look forward to”

To feel excited and positive about something that will happen in the future.

- *She looks forward to her Sunday morning run every week.*
- *We're really looking forward to the long holiday weekend next month.*

## 📚 Vocabulary to Remember

- **hiking** — The activity of walking long distances through nature, usually on paths or trails.
  - *They spent the whole morning hiking through the national park and saw amazing views.*
- **weekend plans** — Activities or events you intend to do on Saturday and Sunday.
  - *Do you have any weekend plans, or are you free to meet up for coffee?*
- **trail** — A path through a natural area used for walking, running, or hiking.
  - *The trail wound through the pine forest and led to a beautiful waterfall.*
- **outdoors** — Outside, in the open air and natural environment, as opposed to inside a building.
  - *She prefers being outdoors to sitting at her desk on sunny days.*
- **leisure activity** — Something you do for fun and relaxation during your free time, not for work.
  - *Hiking is his favorite leisure activity; he goes every chance he gets.*
- **go + gerund** — The standard English pattern for sport and outdoor activities: go hiking, go swimming, go cycling. Never add 'to' between 'go' and the gerund form.
  - *Let's go cycling along the river path this Saturday afternoon.*

## 🔧 Say It Better

1. ❌ *I will go to hiking with my friend.*
   ✅ **I will go hiking with my friend.**
   💡 Use 'go + gerund' for leisure and sport activities (go hiking, go swimming, go shopping) — never insert 'to' before the gerund.

**Q2.** Crowded shopping malls are really not ____ — I find them exhausting after ten minutes.

- my cup of tea
- look forward to
- leisure activity

<details><summary>Show answer</summary><p><strong>my cup of tea</strong> — 'My cup of tea' means something that suits your personal taste and slots in perfectly after 'not'; the other options are a verb phrase and a noun phrase that cannot fill this slot grammatically.</p></details>

**Q3.** He always ____ the annual team retreat because it's a chance to relax and reconnect.

- looks forward to
- hiking
- outdoors

<details><summary>Show answer</summary><p><strong>looks forward to</strong> — 'Look forward to' is a verb phrase meaning to anticipate eagerly; 'hiking' and 'outdoors' are not verb phrases and cannot replace a verb in this sentence.</p></details>

**Q4.** Have you decided on any ____ yet, or is your Saturday still completely open?

- weekend plans
- leisure activity
- hiking

<details><summary>Show answer</summary><p><strong>weekend plans</strong> — 'Weekend plans' refers to things you intend to do on Saturday or Sunday and fits naturally after 'decided on any'; the other options don't work as noun phrases for scheduled activities on a specific day.</p></details>

**Q5.** Every Sunday she goes ____ along the hill path behind her house to clear her head.

- hiking
- outdoors
- leisure activity

<details><summary>Show answer</summary><p><strong>hiking</strong> — 'Go hiking' is the correct 'go + gerund' pattern for this sport activity; 'outdoors' is a place word that describes where you are, not what you do, and 'leisure activity' is a noun phrase that cannot follow 'goes'.</p></details>
