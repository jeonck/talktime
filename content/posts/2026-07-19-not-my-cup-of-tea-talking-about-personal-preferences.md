## Today's Idiom

> **not my cup of tea**

This lesson is anchored on the classic idiom 'not my cup of tea' and two related expressions for discussing personal likes and dislikes. Learners explore key vocabulary for talking about preferences politely and confidently, and practice avoiding the most common mistakes intermediate speakers make when using these phrases.

## 💬 Idioms

### “not my cup of tea”

something you personally do not enjoy or find appealing, without judging others who do

- *I tried watching horror films, but they're really not my cup of tea.*
- *Camping in the rain might be fun for some people, but it's definitely not my cup of tea.*

### “an acquired taste”

something people often dislike at first but gradually come to enjoy after being exposed to it repeatedly

- *Blue cheese is an acquired taste — I hated it as a child but love it now.*
- *His experimental jazz is an acquired taste, but the fans who get it are very passionate.*

### “to each their own”

a phrase meaning everyone has the right to their own preferences, even if you disagree with them

- *I don't understand the appeal of reality TV, but to each their own.*
- *She puts pineapple on her pizza — to each their own, I suppose.*

## 📚 Vocabulary to Remember

- **preference** — a greater liking for one thing over another
  - *My preference is for quiet evenings at home rather than loud parties.*
- **appealing** — attractive or interesting in a way that makes you want to try or have something
  - *The idea of a weekend road trip sounds very appealing to me.*
- **pastime** — an activity done regularly for enjoyment during free time; a hobby
  - *Gardening is a popular pastime for people who enjoy spending time outdoors.*
- **enthusiast** — a person who is very interested in and excited about a particular activity or subject
  - *He is a coffee enthusiast who visits a different café every weekend.*
- **keen on** — very interested in or enthusiastic about something
  - *She's very keen on learning new languages and studies Spanish every morning.*
- **genre** — a category or type, especially of art, music, film, or literature
  - *Science fiction is my favorite genre — I read it constantly.*
- **indifferent** — having no particular interest in or feeling for something; neither liking nor disliking it
  - *He seemed completely indifferent to the outcome of the game.*
- **broaden one's horizons** — to expand the range of experiences, knowledge, or tastes you have
  - *Trying food from different cultures is a great way to broaden your horizons.*

## 🔧 Say It Better

1. ❌ *This movie is not my cup.*
   ✅ **This movie is not my cup of tea.**
   💡 The full fixed phrase is 'cup of tea' — dropping 'of tea' makes the idiom incomplete and unrecognizable.
2. ❌ *It's not my cup of tea for me.*
   ✅ **It's not my cup of tea.**
   💡 'For me' is already implied by 'my', so adding it creates redundancy.
3. ❌ *I am not my cup of tea about jazz.*
   ✅ **Jazz is not my cup of tea.**
   💡 The thing you dislike is the subject of the idiom — say '[thing] is not my cup of tea', not 'I am not my cup of tea about [thing]'.
4. ❌ *Horror movies is not my cup of tea.*
   ✅ **Horror movies are not my cup of tea.**
   💡 Plural subject ('horror movies') requires the plural verb 'are', not 'is'.
5. ❌ *It's not my cup of tea, but I can to understand why people like it.*
   ✅ **It's not my cup of tea, but I can understand why people like it.**
   💡 Modal verbs like 'can' are always followed by the bare infinitive — no 'to'.
6. ❌ *This music genre not my cup of tea.*
   ✅ **This music genre is not my cup of tea.**
   💡 Don't omit the linking verb 'is' — English sentences always need a verb.
7. ❌ *I don't have acquired taste for spicy food.*
   ✅ **Spicy food is an acquired taste for me.**
   💡 'Acquired taste' describes the food itself, not a quality the person possesses; also needs the article 'an'.
8. ❌ *To each their own, so we should respect other's preference.*
   ✅ **To each their own, so we should respect others' preferences.**
   💡 Use 'others'' (plural possessive) and 'preferences' (plural) when speaking about people in general.

## ✅ Check Yourself

**Q1.** I tried watching golf on TV last night, but honestly it's just ____.

- not my cup of tea
- an acquired taste
- to each their own

<details><summary>Show answer</summary><p><strong>not my cup of tea</strong> — 'Not my cup of tea' expresses the speaker's personal dislike; 'an acquired taste' implies it could grow on you, and 'to each their own' is a standalone comment about others, not a personal feeling.</p></details>

**Q2.** Dark chocolate seems very bitter the first time you eat it, but it's ____; most daily chocolate lovers end up preferring it.

- not my cup of tea
- an acquired taste
- keen on

<details><summary>Show answer</summary><p><strong>an acquired taste</strong> — 'An acquired taste' describes something disliked at first but enjoyed with repeated exposure — exactly the pattern the sentence describes.</p></details>

**Q3.** She loves heavy metal and I prefer classical — but ____, right?

- to each their own
- indifferent
- an acquired taste

<details><summary>Show answer</summary><p><strong>to each their own</strong> — 'To each their own' is the right phrase for acknowledging that different people can have different valid preferences without either being wrong.</p></details>

**Q4.** He doesn't seem ____ to which restaurant we choose — he said anything is fine.

- indifferent
- appealing
- enthusiast

<details><summary>Show answer</summary><p><strong>indifferent</strong> — 'Indifferent to' means having no strong feeling either way, which matches 'anything is fine'; 'appealing' is an adjective that doesn't fit here, and 'enthusiast' is a noun, not a description of attitude.</p></details>

**Q5.** Living abroad for a year is a wonderful chance to ____ and experience new ways of life.

- broaden one's horizons
- pastime
- preference

<details><summary>Show answer</summary><p><strong>broaden one's horizons</strong> — 'Broaden one's horizons' is the only verb phrase here and means exactly what the sentence describes; 'pastime' and 'preference' are nouns that cannot follow 'to' in this structure.</p></details>

**Q6.** Mystery novels are her favorite ____; she reads at least two a month.

- genre
- pastime
- enthusiast

<details><summary>Show answer</summary><p><strong>genre</strong> — 'Genre' means a category of literature, which is what the sentence is describing; 'pastime' refers to a hobby activity, and 'enthusiast' is a person, not a type of book.</p></details>

> Today my colleague invited me to join the office hiking club, and I had to be honest — long mountain treks in the heat are really **not my cup of tea**. Walking home alone through the park, though, I started to wonder whether I was being too quick to say no. Maybe hiking is **an acquired taste**, and I simply haven't given it a real chance yet.
